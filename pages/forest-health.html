2027-2030 outlook

Model-based projection (demonstration)
  • Northern Aroostook towns projected to stay High as the outbreak front advances south.

  • Red spruce and balsam fir above ~600 m flagged for top-kill risk and priority sampling.

Outlook and recommended actions

  • Continue annual overwintering larval (L2) sampling at high- and moderate-risk towns ahead of the spring feeding period.
  • Prioritize repeat LiDAR and canopy-change analysis in fir-dominated stands most vulnerable to top-kill.
  • Flag towns with consecutive years of rising defoliation for ground verification.
  • Favor thinning that reduces fir dominance and increases diversity to lower outbreak susceptibility.

Most sensitive site types

Sandy outwash Shallow till Ledge / thin soil South-facing slopes

Outlook and recommended actions

  • Track soil-moisture and stream-flow anomalies through the growing season at moderate- and severe-drought towns.
  • Prioritize repeat LiDAR canopy-change analysis on sandy outwash and shallow-soil stands most prone to dieback.
  • Flag regeneration and recently thinned stands, which are most exposed during extended dry spells.
  • Where management is planned, retain shade and favor drought-tolerant species mixes on droughty sites.
