Spruce budworm monitoring and outlook

⚠ Illustrative demonstration data - the spruce budworm figures below are sample values for interface preview only and are not based on field surveys.

The eastern spruce budworm (Choristoneura fumiferana) is the most damaging defoliating insect of spruce-fir forests in the Northeast. Larvae feed on the new foliage of balsam fir and spruce, and sustained outbreaks can cause widespread growth loss and mortality. DigiSylva combines field plots with repeat LiDAR to track the canopy change that may signal budworm defoliation.

Outlook and recommended actions

  • Continue annual overwintering larval (L2) sampling at high- and moderate-risk sites ahead of the spring feeding period.
  • Prioritize repeat LiDAR and canopy-change analysis in stands with more than 40% balsam fir, which are most vulnerable to top-kill.
  • Flag plots with two or more consecutive years of rising defoliation for ground verification.
  • Where management is planned, favor thinning that reduces fir dominance and increases stand diversity to lower outbreak susceptibility.
